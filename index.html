Gallery of Grief

Of Ink and Shade

When mirth did pale and men wore borrowed skin,
I stitched my soul in parchment thin,
Where hearts, unheeded, whispered low—
The only truth was written woe.

Where My Sorrows Dwell

When all did feign, and hearts took flight,
The silence grew in deeper night,
And there within the shadow’s hem,
My pen became my diadem.

Hollow Throne

I crowned the ache, I ruled the rain,
I wrote in blood the weight of pain,
The verse alone could ever know
The madness I refused to show.

Lunatic’s Ink

“I would sooner drown in the abyss of madness than love again—yet am I not already lost? For what sane soul bleeds ink into verse and calls the wound a poem?”
“To write is to betray the silence with a scream too beautiful to be heard.”
